## 1. Redmark

**Etymology & Positioning:** "Redmark" evokes the traditional "redlining" process central to contract review, where attorneys mark up documents with changes and comments. 
The term "redline" is deeply embedded in legal practice, referring to the critical process of reviewing and marking contract revisions
. The name suggests precision, attention to detail, and the decisive action of marking what matters. For mid-market law firms handling 
contract reviews that can take over two hours per contract just to locate specific clauses
, "Redmark" positions the startup as the modern evolution of this essential practice.

**Availability:** 
Coined terms typically avoid common trademark conflicts
. Redmark.com likely available - unique legal term with no major brand conflicts.

small to mid-size firms that benefit from clause libraries and risk detection features
